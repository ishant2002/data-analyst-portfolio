Project Overview

This project was designed for finance and operations teams that process large volumes of vendor bills, reimbursement claims, or scanned invoices. Instead of manually reviewing each document, the system extracts important fields using OCR, standardizes the data, and applies validation checks to surface potential fraud indicators.

The final workflow acts like a first-level review layer: it does not replace human judgment, but it helps teams prioritize risky bills and reduce time spent checking low-risk documents.

Business Problem

Manual bill verification is slow, inconsistent, and difficult to audit. Finance teams often need to check vendor names, invoice numbers, dates, totals, tax values, duplicate bills, and unusual amount patterns across many files. When the process depends on spreadsheets and manual review, suspicious bills can be missed or discovered too late.

Solution Built

  • OCR extraction layer to read invoice number, vendor, bill date, amount, tax, and line-item details.
  • Data cleaning pipeline to standardize vendor names, currency values, date formats, and missing fields.
  • Fraud rules for duplicate invoice numbers, repeated amounts, weekend billing, unusual spikes, and vendor mismatch checks.
  • Risk scoring logic that assigns each bill a review priority based on combined anomaly signals.
  • Reviewer output file with extracted fields, validation results, risk level, and recommended action.

Advanced Workflow

The system follows a staged pipeline: document intake, OCR extraction, field normalization, duplicate detection, vendor validation, anomaly scoring, and final reviewer output. Each stage is designed so finance users can trace why a bill was flagged instead of receiving a black-box result.

  • Confidence checks for extracted invoice fields to separate clean records from low-quality scans.
  • Vendor normalization logic to match spelling variations, abbreviations, and repeated supplier names.
  • Risk categories for duplicate invoice numbers, unusual amounts, mismatched tax values, and repeated billing patterns.
